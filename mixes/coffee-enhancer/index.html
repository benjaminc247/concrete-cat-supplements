Coffee Enhancer

This supplement enhances the benefits of caffeine while counteracting the side effects.

Mix one scoop (700mg) with every 8oz coffee.

  • Increases alertness, attention, and mental stamina over caffeine alone.
  • Counteracts anxiety, jitters, sleeplessness, and post-caffeine crash.
  • Fights caffeine addiction, reducing tolerance and withdrawals.

https://examine.com/supplements/theanine/

Benefits and drawbacks are mixed when taken alone, but when taken with caffeine, negative affects of each are counteracted by the other, and positive affects are magnified.

When taken with caffeine:
Improves measures of attention, reaction time, working memory, and mental fatigue over caffeine alone
Reduces measures of stress and anxiety that are increased when taking caffeine alone
Counteracts increased blood pressure and reduced sleep quality caused by caffeine alone

Safety:
Non-essential amino acid not produced by the body
Naturally occurring in tea ~14mg/8oz
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27019564/
No adverse affects have been observed in any study, including animal studies using doses 640x maximum doses used in human studies
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/16759779/
Studies focusing on safety in humans are lacking

Dosage:
Most studies between 100-400mg
Theanine:caffeine ratios - tea 1:3, studies between 1:1 and 2:1, most supplements 2:1

https://examine.com/supplements/l-tyrosine/

Studies to prove effectiveness are lacking, but mechanism of action and anecdotal results are strong. Tyrosine has become ubiquitous in pre-workouts and combining with caffeine is promising.

Caffeine stimulates production of catecholamines dopamine and norepinephrine. Tyrosine is a precursor to those catecholamines and may become depleted, this seems to be involved in post-caffeine crash and in caffeine withdrawals.

Reduced impairment in a stressful environment:
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/2736402/

Enhanced cognitive performance when dopamine and norepinephrine have been depleted:
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26424423/
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/25797188/

Improved focus, attention, and reaction time when Tyrosine is paired with caffeine and theanine:
https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6880365/

Anecdotally, pairing Tyrosine with caffeine has several results:
Enhances the 'euphoria' feeling from caffeine, most likely due to allowing for higher dopamine production.
Reduces post-caffeine crash, most likely by preventing short time deficiency.
Reduces caffeine tolerance, dependence, and withdrawals, most likely by preventing long term deficiency.

Safety:
Non essential amino acid produced by the body
Naturally occurring in many foods
RDI ~875mg/day
Doses of up to 10g used in human studies with no adverse affects
https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/29168741/

Dosage:
Doses used in studies are very high, minimum effective dosage has not been established.
Anecdotally 1.5-2g/day seems to be effective and is what is used in most supplements
